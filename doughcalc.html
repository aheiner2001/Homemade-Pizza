Dough Making Instructions

By hand / Stand mixer

  1. Cool water to below 60°F (15°C).
  2. Mix the water and yeast until dissolved.
  3. Add the flour and olive oil; mix on low for 2 minutes to combine.
  4. Continue mixing on low speed, then add sugar and salt.
  5. Mix for another 8–10 minutes until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.
  6. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for 1–3 hours at room temperature for bulk fermentation.
  7. Divide the dough into portions based on the number of pizzas (see quantity).
  8. Shape each portion into a tight ball, sealing the seam.
  9. Place dough balls in a lightly oiled container.
  10. Cover tightly and refrigerate for 2–4 days (3 days is ideal) for cold fermentation.
  11. Bring dough to room temperature before stretching and baking.

With bread maker

  1. Add liquids (water, olive oil) to the bread machine pan first.
  2. Add dry ingredients (flour, sugar, salt); create a small well in the center.
  3. Add yeast last — on top of the flour so it stays dry until the machine starts.
  4. Select the dough or pizza-dough cycle. If your machine has separate mix and knead cycles, use mix first then knead.
  5. When the cycle completes, follow the hand/mixer instructions from step 6 onward: rest 1–3 hours, divide, shape, and refrigerate for cold fermentation if desired (2–4 days).
